  Can I use the SIx Driver and DBFNTX in the same application?

  Absolutely!  In fact, if you don't link SIXCDX.OBJ or SIXNSX.OBJ (which
  makes the SIx Driver the default RDD) into your application, DBFNTX still
  gets included.  Both RDD's are available to your application for opening,
  reading, writing, creating, and all other operations with databases and
  indexes.

  However, the SIx Driver 3.0 comes with a DBFNTX replacement called SIXNTX.
  If you need .DBF/.DBT and/or .NTX index compatibility in the same applica-
  tion as SIXCDX and/or SIXNSX, your load size will be much less if you use
  SIXNTX instead of DBFNTX.
