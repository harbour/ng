 Local Area Network

     (network) A system by which microcomputers are connected (via coaxial
     cable, optical fiber, twisted pair phone wire, or other media), relying
     on sophisticated operating software to perform such functions as file
     and peripheral sharing, electronic mail, and centralized backup of data.
